# Folder Permissions: Tips & Tricks for Portal Management

By default, clients have access to all portal content. You can use **granular permissions** to restrict access to individual folders or pages. For example, one client can have access only to Folder 1 and the other client can have access only to Folder 2, and both clients are unaware of other folders in the portal.

[Embedded content](https://www.youtube.com/embed/7sWSjTNAhTY?feature=oembed)

## General Information [#](#b3502767312_5144)

### Who can manage access to portal content? [#](#b3502767312_5092)

Any member with access to the **Portal Customizer** can manage portal permissions.

### Who can restrict access to specific content in the portal? [#](#b3502767312_5173)

Any portal member, except those with access to the **Portal Customizer**, can restrict access.

Read more about managing portal access: [**HOW DO I MANAGE PORTAL ACCESS?**](https://thefusebase.com/guides/client-portal/access/)

## What Portal Content Can I Restrict? [#](#b3502767312_5241)

You can restrict access to all root items in the **Portal Sidebar**:

-   Folders
-   Root pages
-   Root links

Restricting access to a folder automatically restricts access to all subfolders and pages within it. You cannot restrict access to an individual page or subfolder within a folder.

![](https://thefusebase.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/image-8-1024x557.png)

You cannot restrict access to:

-   Home page
-   Portal’s footer and top bar content

## Changing Folder Permissions [#](#b3502767312_6713)

### How do I prevent certain members from accessing folders? [#](#b3502767312_6647)

1) Go to the **Portal’s Customizer**.

2) Hover over the required item and click the menu. Select the “**Manage Access”** button:

![](https://thefusebase.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/image-13-1024x471.png)

3) In the window that appears, you will see a list of members with access to the folder.

4) Click the “**Block access**” icon next to the user you want to restrict.

![](https://thefusebase.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Folder-Permissions-4.png)

5) Confirm your action.

### How do I allow members to access a specific folder? [#](#b3502767312_1877)

If you have restricted access to folders and want to allow individual members to access them, here are the steps to follow:

1) Go to the **Portal Customizer**.

2) Hover over the folder you want to share and click on the menu. Then choose “**Manage Access**“

3) Click on “**Invite**“:

![](https://thefusebase.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Folder-Permissions-5.png)

4) To grant access to the folder, click “**No access”** and replace it with “**Access granted”**. The folder and its contents will be visible to users with this status:

![](https://thefusebase.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Folder-Permissions-6.png)

5) Save your changes:

![](https://thefusebase.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Folder-Permissions-7.png)

If the member you invite to the portal is not already a workspace member, they will be granted access to the workspace **automatically**.

## How To View The Portal From The Client Side [#](#b3502767312_2633)

If you’ve restricted access to specific areas, you’ll probably want to view the portal from the client’s perspective. To do this, go to “**Preview”** mode.

![](https://thefusebase.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/image-11-1024x524.png)

Select the desired client from the “**Preview as**” drop-down list. The portal view specific to this user will then be displayed.

![](https://thefusebase.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/image-12-1024x387.png)

## Inviting New Users To The Folder [#](#b3502767312_8990)

To invite a new user directly to a portal folder, follow these steps:

1) Go to the **Portal’s Customizer**.

2) Hover over the folder you want to share and click on the menu. Then choose “**Manage Access**“

![](https://thefusebase.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/image-14-1024x471.png)

3) Click on “**Invite**“:

![](https://thefusebase.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Folder-Permissions-11.png)

4) Go to the “**By Email**” tab.

5) Select a role and enter the user’s email address.

![](https://thefusebase.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Folder-Permissions-12.png)

6) Click on “**Send magic link**“.

![](https://thefusebase.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Folder-Permissions-13.png)

Once the user receives the invitation, they will have access to the following:

-   Home page
-   The folder to which they have been invited
-   Portal’s footer and top bar content

When you invite clients to your portal using the “**Invite Clients**” option, they can only see the open-access folders/pages. However, you can manually grant permissions to specific folders you previously limited. This rule doesn’t apply to members who have access to the **Portal Customizer** based on their role.

## A new folder is available to everyone by default. Can this be restricted? [#](#9-toc-title)

Yes, of course. When creating a folder, you can make it available only to portal managers.

![](https://thefusebase.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/image-15-1024x624.png)

Later, you can simply invite the required clients to this folder.

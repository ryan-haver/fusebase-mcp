# Video

With [FuseBase](https://thefusebase.com/) you can add video files to a page both through the attachment panel and the editor. You can also insert videos into tables.

Add and play videos directly in FuseBase with Embed. FuseBase supports such services as Youtube, Vimeo, Vivo, etc.

## Add video file [#](#b3190428781_13)

### Create a video block [#](#b3190428781_394)

-   To create a video block, click on the **+** in the editor and select **File** or use **cmd/ctrl** + **shift** + **f**.
-   Upload video files from your computer or drag and drop them.

With [FuseBase Pro AI](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/pljdghaomjmmleolekcgcamfpbhekbmc), you can record videos and save them in your page. To make the page and video look more personalized, we have added a couple of options to customize video players.

### Drop files, resize and move blocks [#](#b3463009348_46)

-   Drag and drop files into the editor.
-   Move files to columns by dragging them.
-   Click and drag the block to the right/left, or up/down.

-   Click on the paperclip icon and you will see the attachments panel.
-   Upload video files from your computer or drag and drop them.

### Why did I get a message saying I can’t play my video? [#](#b1338734181_1617)

![Why did I get a message saying I can't play my video?](https://app.nimbusweb.me/box/attachment/4485395/0g188gi48sxnkqz1aaxx/OIsDD4yYT4x0wbVg/Group%20170.png)

Most likely, your video is encoded by the H.265 codec, which is not supported by most browsers, including Google Chrome and Mozilla Firefox.

#### What should I do now? [#](#b1338734181_1683)

At the moment, the only option is to convert the video to the H.264 codec and upload it again into a page.  
You can do this through this online service – [https://anyconv.com/h265-to-h264-converter/](https://anyconv.com/h265-to-h264-converter/)  
You can also use the Freemake desktop converter – [hhttps://www.freemake.com/free\_video\_converter/](https://www.freemake.com/free_video_converter/)

## Video file menu [#](#b138215659_85)

### Video settings [#](#b1338734181_31)

-   Click on the three dots **menu** next to the block.
-   Select **Video settings**.
-   Then select the option you need.

**Autoplay** – enable or disable autoplay when it is in the visible area.

**Sound by default** – the ability to turn off the default sound. Works great with autoplay function.

**Loop** – if you need looping “endless” playback then this option is for you.

**Show control**s – the ability to disable video control tools and the line with the video name.

**Shadow** – allows you to add shadow to the video file.

**How to make shadow effect for images or videos**

In FuseBase, you can highlight not only text, but also pictures and video. This is possible with the help of Shadow effect. Just click on the picture or video menu, and in the Style menu activate the Shadow option.

If you don’t want to change the settings one by one, then turn on the **Motion mode** and you will have a zingy video in one click. It looks great on the landing page and in the knowledge base.

Option control is available through the video menu – Video parameters.

## Adding video files to tables [#](#b138215659_1014)

In FuseBase, you can add videos to tables. To do this, you need to create a table.

-   Click on the table icon or use **cmd/ctrl** + **⌥/alt** + **t**.
-   Select **Attachment** as the type of column or cell.
-   Upload a video file from your computer or drag and drop it.

## Adding video through Embed [#](#b2404600722_395)

With the embed function, you can insert links from popular sites including YouTube, Vimeo, Vivo, SoundCloud, and more.

-   Click on the arrow and select **Embed** or use **cmd/ctrl** + **⌥/alt** + **m**.
-   Paste or type the URL.

### Convert URL into video embed [#](#b2033894131_207)

-   Paste the URL into a page.
-   Convert to Embed or use **cmd/ctrl** \+ **⌥/alt** + **m**.

### Play a video in full screen [#](#b2033894131_246)

If it’s a YouTube embed, click the square icon on the right below the video.
